Swiggy Lunchbox

2023-02-05

Creating a seamless delivery scheduling system for working professionals #

Project type: Personal Project Duration: 3 weeks

Brief #

A large percentage of Swiggy’s most active users are office goers who live alone and order lunch daily from Swiggy. In a recent customer survey of this audience set, Swiggy found that these users often forget to order lunch in time and find it cumbersome and irritating to go through the entire process of ordering food on a daily basis. Remembering to order lunch in time, choosing a restaurant, deciding dishes, deciding the delivery location, payment process, etc. There is a lot to do on a daily basis.

Problem Statement #

Design a concept feature that will allow the users to overcome this problem. Since you are designing this as a part of Swiggy and not something from scratch, keep in mind the existing design system of Swiggy - typography, colors, layout, spacing, variety of elements, etc. and make sure it does not spoil the existing experience of the product.

Introducing Swiggy Lunchbox #

#

Flow for scheduling order #

What after scheduling food? #

  1. The order is not placed at the time of scheduling, instead, they would get an approx. the amount for the food meal which they scheduled for. ( Approximate because delivery charges may change depending on traffic/weather/miscellaneous reasons.)
  2. The users will get a notification about the meal 90 minutes before the scheduled time.
  3. They get 5 minutes to get to edit/approve the scheduled order and the order would be placed.
  4. The actual amount would be debited from the approx. the amount which the users paid, the remaining amount ( if any) would be credited to their bank account.

Before designing the home page, I kept these things in mind:

  1. How to help users find the best economical food options.

Food options > Restaurant options #

In the current Swiggy app, the home screen is created to find the best restaurant options near the user. This works perfectly in the context of the Swiggy app.

For Lunchbox, food options should be prioritized because:

  1. In the research, I find out that people are more focused on the price and the food they want to eat.

For example - If someone wants to eat Poha, the priority would be the cost and the ratings of the food over which restaurant

Categorizations of cuisines and food options #

In the research, when I asked users how they decide what dishes they want to eat, they answered in keywords like - Homemade food, and South Indian food. Creating categories or lists of food items would help them choose their meals faster.

Rescheduling the order #

Users will get 5 minutes before they can reschedule/cancel the order. They can reschedule dates, and days, they can also add/remove food options.

Notifying users about the order üîî #

There are several notification tools you can use to notify the users, whether it is mail, phone, messages, notifications, etc. but I would like to pick Push notifications, Phone calls, and Whatsapp.

  • Phone calls and Whatapp > Messages
    1. Let’s be honest, messages are now just used for copying OTPs, and many companies are switching to Whatsapp to notify users.
    2. The reason is simple, the messaging app is really cluttered with spam, promotions, and OTPs, users miss out on the notifications very easily.

  • Push notifications
  1. Push notifications are one of the best ways to grab the attention of the users, they are utilized by food delivery apps like Swiggy and Zomato for better conversions.
  2. In the context of this case study, we can send users push notifications for approval for the scheduled delivery and a remainder for a day-before before their scheduled order.
